# Metaphors — Chapter 8

A scholarly catalogue of the metaphors in Chapter 8 of *The Great Gatsby* —
Gatsby's last confidences and his death. Each entry quotes the figurative
language and notes what is being compared (tenor → vehicle). Genuine metaphors
are listed first; notable similes follow.

## Metaphors

- **"a fog-horn was groaning incessantly on the Sound"** — the fog-horn personified, groaning.
- **"'Jay Gatsby' had broken up like glass against Tom's hard malice"** — the constructed self as glass shattered.
- **"the long secret extravaganza was played out"** — Gatsby's life-illusion as a theatrical show now ended.
- **"always with indiscernible barbed wire between"** — class separation as invisible barbed wire.
- **"There was a ripe mystery about it, a hint of bedrooms upstairs more beautiful and cool ..."** — Daisy's house ripened with mystery.
- **"the invisible cloak of his uniform might slip from his shoulders"** — the wartime anonymity that protects Gatsby as a magic cloak.
- **"he had committed himself to the following of a grail"** — Daisy as the Holy Grail; love as a sacred quest.
- **"She vanished into her rich house, into her rich, full life, leaving Gatsby—nothing."** — Daisy as something that "vanishes," leaving an absence.
- **"the youth and mystery that wealth imprisons and preserves"** — wealth as a preserving prison.
- **"Daisy, gleaming like silver, safe and proud above the hot struggles of the poor"** — Daisy as gleaming silver elevated above the poor.
- **"her artificial world was redolent of orchids and pleasant, cheerful snobbery and orchestras which set the rhythm of the year"** — society as a perfumed, music-timed world.
- **"All night the saxophones wailed the hopeless comment of the 'Beale Street Blues'"** — the music personified, "wailing" commentary.
- **"this low sweet fever"** — the social season as a fever.
- **"fresh faces drifted here and there like rose petals blown by the sad horns"** — partygoers as windblown petals.
- **"Through this twilight universe Daisy began to move again"** — her social world as a twilight universe.
- **"something within her was crying for a decision"** — inner need personified.
- **"That force took shape in the middle of spring with the arrival of Tom Buchanan."** — Tom as the embodiment of an abstract "force."
- **"the sun ... seemed to spread itself in benediction over the vanishing city"** — sunset as a priestly blessing.
- **"He stretched out his hand desperately as if to snatch only a wisp of air, to save a fragment of the spot that she had made lovely"** — the irrecoverable past as a wisp of air.
- **"he must have felt that he had lost the old warm world, paid a high price for living too long with a single dream"** — the dream as a costly purchase; the world as "warm."
- **"A new world, material without being real, where poor ghosts, breathing dreams like air, drifted fortuitously about"** — post-illusion reality as a ghost-world.
- **"like that ashen, fantastic figure gliding toward him through the amorphous trees"** — Wilson as an ash-figure (linking him to the valley of ashes).
- **"how raw the sunlight was upon the scarcely created grass"** — the world rendered newly, rawly created.
- **"tracing, like the leg of compass, a thin red circle in the water"** — the death-mattress drawing a compass-circle of blood.
- **"the holocaust was complete"** — the murder-suicide named a holocaust (a burnt sacrifice).
- **"a token of forgotten violence"** (Cody's photograph, recalled) — the portrait as a relic of violence.

## Notable similes and related figurative language

- "broken up **like** glass" — the Gatsby persona.
- "gleaming **like** silver" — Daisy.
- "**like** rose petals blown by the sad horns" — the dancers.
- "breathing dreams **like** air" — the ghosts of the new world.
- "**like** the leg of compass" — the mattress's blood-circle.
- "We pushed aside curtains that were **like** pavilions" — the grand, musty rooms.
- "her voice came over the wire as something fresh and cool **as if** a divot from a green golf links had come sailing in at the office window" — Jordan's voice.
- "musty as though they hadn't been aired for many days" — the abandoned house.
