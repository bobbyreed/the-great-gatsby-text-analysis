# Metaphors — Chapter 3

A scholarly catalogue of the metaphors in Chapter 3 of *The Great Gatsby*. Each
entry quotes the figurative language and notes what is being compared (tenor →
vehicle). Genuine metaphors are listed first; notable similes follow.

## Metaphors

- **"In his blue gardens men and girls came and went like moths among the whisperings and the champagne and the stars"** — Gatsby's guests as moths drawn to light (image carried by the simile "like moths").
- **"his station wagon scampered like a brisk yellow bug"** — the car as a scurrying insect.
- **"the air is alive with chatter and laughter and casual innuendo"** — the atmosphere given life.
- **"the opera of voices pitches a key higher"** — the crowd's talk as opera/music.
- **"Laughter is easier ... spilled with prodigality, tipped out at a cheerful word"** — laughter as a liquid spilled.
- **"glide on through the sea-change of faces and voices and color"** — the shifting party as a sea.
- **"a whole pitful of oboes and trombones..."** — the orchestra as an orchestra pit teeming.
- **"The lights grow brighter as the earth lurches away from the sun"** — sunset rendered as the planet's lurching motion.
- **"the orchestra is playing yellow cocktail music"** — synesthetic metaphor: music given color.
- **"floating in the Sound was a triangle of silver scales"** — moonlight on water as fish-scales.
- **"the moon had risen higher ... trembling a little to the stiff, tinny drip of the banjoes"** — banjo notes as dripping liquid.
- **"the premature moon, produced ... out of a caterer's basket"** — the moon as catered party-prop.
- **"the whole caravansary"** (and the party run "according to the rules ... associated with amusement parks") — Gatsby's house as inn/amusement park.
- **"that they were theirs for a few words in the right key"** — opportunity as something unlockable by the right musical key.
- **"It was one of those rare smiles with a quality of eternal reassurance in it"** — Gatsby's smile as a reassuring presence (extended into near-personification: "It faced ... It understood you ... believed in you").
- **"a dim background started to take shape behind him"** — Gatsby's mysterious past as a stage backdrop.
- **"the echolalia of the garden"** — the party chatter as meaningless repeated sound.
- **"happy vacuous bursts of laughter rose toward the summer sky"** — laughter as rising matter.
- **"their faces ... blurred ... and pursued the rest of their way in slow black rivulets"** — running mascara/tears as black streams.
- **"a deep vinous sleep"** — sleep colored/saturated by wine.
- **"she appeared suddenly at his side like an angry diamond"** — the wife as a hard, glittering jewel.
- **"the white chasms of lower New York"** — the skyscraper canyons as chasms.
- **"the enchanted metropolitan twilight"** — the city dusk as enchantment.
- **"romantic women ... faded through a door into warm darkness"** — imagined women dissolving like apparitions.
- **"a phrase began to beat in my ears"** — a phrase given a pulse.
- **"this was because she felt safer on a plane where any divergence from a code would be thought impossible"** — moral conduct mapped as a level "plane."
- **"interior rules that act as brakes on my desires"** — self-restraint as a car's brakes.
- **"a faint mustache of perspiration"** — sweat as a mustache.
- **"this is mine: I am one of the few honest people that I have ever known"** — honesty claimed as a possessed "cardinal virtue."

## Notable similes and related figurative language

- "men and girls came and went **like** moths" — the guests as moths.
- "his station wagon scampered **like** a brisk yellow bug" — the car as bug.
- "she ... melted into her party at the door" — Jordan dissolving into the crowd.
- "appeared suddenly at his side **like** an angry diamond" — the jealous wife as a diamond.
- "champagne ... in glasses bigger than finger bowls" — comic hyperbole.
- "as if she had first learned to walk upon golf courses on clean, crisp mornings" — Jordan's jaunty carriage.
- "yawned gracefully in my face" — Jordan's affected ennui.
