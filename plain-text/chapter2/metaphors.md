# Metaphors — Chapter 2

A scholarly catalogue of the metaphors in Chapter 2 of *The Great Gatsby*. Each
entry quotes the figurative language and notes what is being compared (tenor →
vehicle). Genuine metaphors are listed first; notable similes follow.

## Metaphors

- **"This is a valley of ashes—a fantastic farm where ashes grow like wheat into ridges and hills"** — the industrial wasteland as a grotesque farm, ash as crop. (The governing metaphor of the chapter.)
- **"ashes take the forms of houses and chimneys ... and finally ... of men who move dimly and already crumbling through the powdery air"** — the ash-grey laborers as ash-men, half-formed and disintegrating.
- **"the eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg"** ... **"brood on over the solemn dumping ground"** — the billboard eyes personified as a brooding, quasi-divine watcher (the chapter's central symbol).
- **"the spasms of bleak dust"** — drifting dust given convulsive, bodily motion.
- **"this shadow of a garage must be a blind"** — the garage as a mere front/screen.
- **"the nerves of her body were continually smouldering"** — Myrtle's vitality as a banked fire.
- **"walking through her husband as if he were a ghost"** — Wilson reduced, metaphorically, to a ghost (carried into simile).
- **"mingling immediately with the cement color of the walls"** — Wilson dissolving into the gray surroundings.
- **"a white ashen dust veiled his dark suit"** — dust as a veil/shroud.
- **"the room grew smaller around her until she seemed to be revolving on a noisy, creaking pivot through the smoky air"** — Myrtle's swelling personality as a body spinning on a mechanical pivot.
- **"a long white cake of apartment houses"** ... **"at one slice"** — the apartment block as a cake to be sliced.
- **"I was within and without, simultaneously enchanted and repelled by the inexhaustible variety of life."** — Nick's doubled vantage figured spatially as inside/outside.
- **"our line of yellow windows must have contributed their share of human secrecy"** — lit windows as repositories of secret human life.
- **"her warm breath poured over me the story of her first meeting with Tom"** — narrative figured as liquid breath being poured.
- **"a wreath with a black silk bow for mother's grave that'll last all summer"** — within Myrtle's list, mourning reduced to a durable consumer good.

## Notable similes and related figurative language

- "ashes grow **like** wheat into ridges and hills" — the founding simile of the valley of ashes.
- "she carried her surplus flesh sensuously **as** some women can" — Myrtle's physicality.
- "walking through her husband **as if** he were a ghost" — Wilson's nullity.
- "almost pastoral ... I wouldn't have been surprised to see a great flock of white sheep turn the corner" — Fifth Avenue imagined as pastureland.
- "The late afternoon sky bloomed in the window for a moment **like** the blue honey of the Mediterranean" — the sky as honey.
- "decomposed apathetically" / "groaning faintly" — the dog biscuit and the dog given human listlessness.
- "Mr. McKee was asleep on a chair ... **like** a photograph of a man of action" — McKee likened, ironically, to his own medium.
- "hovered **like** an ectoplasm on the wall" — the enlarged photograph as a ghostly emanation.
- "entangled in some wild strident argument which pulled me back, **as if with ropes**, into my chair" — the argument as binding ropes.
